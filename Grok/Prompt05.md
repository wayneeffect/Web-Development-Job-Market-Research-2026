**Mid-level jobs in web development** (also called intermediate or mid-senior) typically require **2–5 years of professional experience** (sometimes listed as 3–7 years depending on the company). At this stage, developers move beyond following instructions to independently owning features, making technical decisions, and contributing to team processes.

### Common Mid-Level Job Titles
- **Mid-Level / Intermediate Web Developer**
- **Mid-Level Front-End Developer** (e.g., React Developer)
- **Mid-Level Back-End Developer**
- **Mid-Level Full-Stack Developer** (very common)
- **Software Engineer II** or **Web Application Developer** (mid-level)
- **Front-End Engineer** / **Full-Stack Engineer** (mid-level)

These titles often appear in product companies, digital agencies, SaaS firms, and enterprises.

### Key Responsibilities
- Independently design, implement, and ship features end-to-end.
- Write clean, maintainable, and testable code.
- Integrate APIs, optimize performance, and ensure responsiveness/security.
- Participate in code reviews, architecture discussions, and technical planning.
- Mentor junior developers and collaborate with designers, PMs, and stakeholders.
- Debug complex issues and improve existing codebases.
- Work with modern tools: CI/CD, cloud deployment, databases, etc.

Mid-level devs are expected to handle ambiguity better and deliver business value with less oversight than juniors.

### Required Skills
**Core**:
- Strong **HTML, CSS, JavaScript/TypeScript**
- Proficiency in at least one framework (React/Next.js dominant for front-end; Node.js, Python, etc. for back-end)
- REST/GraphQL APIs, databases (SQL/NoSQL), state management
- Git workflows, testing, responsive design, accessibility

**Differentiators**:
- System design basics and performance optimization
- Cloud services (AWS, Vercel, Firebase)
- DevOps awareness (Docker, CI/CD)
- Ability to review code and suggest improvements

A solid portfolio or GitHub history showing shipped features is essential.

### Salary Expectations (US, 2026)
Mid-level salaries show clear growth from entry-level:

- **Typical range**: $85,000 – $130,000+ base
- **Common averages**: ~$100,000 – $120,000 total compensation (including bonuses/equity)
- Full-stack or specialized roles (e.g., React + Node) often trend higher
- Tech hubs (SF, NY, Seattle) can push $130k–$160k+
- Lower in smaller markets or non-tech companies

These figures vary by location, company size, and exact skills. Total pay frequently exceeds base due to bonuses and stock.

### Mid-Level vs. Junior vs. Senior
- **Junior (0–2 years)**: Implements tasks with guidance, focuses on learning the stack.
- **Mid-Level (2–5/6 years)**: Owns features, contributes to decisions, starts mentoring.
- **Senior (5–8+ years)**: Leads architecture, makes high-impact decisions, mentors teams, handles complex systems.

### How to Reach Mid-Level
- Build and ship real projects or contribute significantly at your current job.
- Deepen one or two specializations (e.g., modern front-end + cloud).
- Improve soft skills: communication, problem-solving, and collaboration.
- Network and update your resume/LinkedIn with quantifiable achievements (e.g., “Improved load time by 40%” or “Led feature X used by 10k users”).

**Bottom line**: Mid-level roles offer good pay, more autonomy, and a stepping stone to senior/lead positions. They are widely available but competitive — success depends on demonstrated impact and versatility rather than just years of experience.
